Introduction to the Project

Overall Requirements

The COMP 1950 owner and instructor (Alistair Calder) would like the current web site redesigned in order to better meet the needs of students. We (Team Persius) have been contracted to provide a redesigned concept for the website. This work also constitutes our final assignment as students enrolled in the winter 2015 COMP 1950 class. The project results will be delivered by construction of a website, which will be turned over to Alistair as our final assignment.

There is no guarantee that our customer, Alistair, will use our concept. However, we can guarantee that our concept will provide ideas and result in feedback to Alistair, from our and other students point of view.

COMP 1950 Project (Final Assignment) Goals

  1. Students must have easy access to notes, examples and other information
  2. As the project is intended to also practice the skills we have learned on the COMP1950 Course, the project site should reflect the aims of the course:
    • Standards compliant web development
    • Valid CSS
    • Valid HTML5
    • Methodology-driven design
    • inclusion of a referer link to allow easy validation of each page on our project site
    • Other applicable skills learned on the course
  3. Students must have easy access to notes, examples and other information

Deliverables

  1. a project site (generally known as a meta site)
    • web site created for this project that describes the project
      • Introduction page that describes the project and the members of the team
      • Roles, responsibilities, and work completed by each team memberm
    • links to various artifacts produced
      • Wireframe(s) of the major sections of the site (Example lecture page) that were used to design the protosite (done in HTML, Word or Visio).
      • Descriptions of what is included in each content area
    • Sitemap
  2. supporting documents for the proposed, redesigned, COMP1950 website
  3. A proto-site that has at least three of the main pages mocked up
    • home page
    • a lecture page
    • blog page (Tumblr must be used)
    • Other pages (optional)
  4. Rationale page
    • describes why we have made the various design decisions that are being proposed by our team for the new COMP 1950 website
